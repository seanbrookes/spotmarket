Hop Farms British Columbia March 2016

I'm not alone in having fond memories of the hop yards that used to line Highway 1 through the Fraser Valley as a kid.

I began to wonder about the hops.  Where did they go, why did they go?  Can they come back?

Turns out yes they can.  There is a less well known movement occurring behind the scenes of the brewery explosion: hop farming is having a renaissance as well.

Today the scale is a little smaller but the dreams are big.  

I spent some time trying to figure out who is leading the charge in the hop growing revival in BC and here is a summary of what I have found to date.

If I missed your hop yard or got something wrong here please contact me and I'll be happy to correct it.

76+ craft breweries in BC require approximately 360,000+ kg of dried hops annually (792,000+ lbs), or the equivalent of 450-500 acres of farmland in full production 

src: http://www.persephonebrewing.com/wp-content/uploads/2015/07/TR1FeasibilityStudy.pdf
